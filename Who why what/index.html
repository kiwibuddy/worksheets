WHO · WHY · WHAT

Identity shapes everything. But the order matters. When the circles are in the wrong order, brokenness drives you. When WHO is at the centre, everything else flows from security.

"When you know your WHO, it defines your WHY, which gives power and righteousness to your WHAT."
WHO — Your Identity
Where is your sense of identity actually coming from right now?
Not the answer you think you should give — the honest one. What is the thing that, if you lost it or failed at it, would shake you most deeply? That's likely where your identity is actually rooted at this moment.
WHY — Your Passions
What are the things that genuinely light you up — the passions, convictions, and desires that feel most truly yours?
Name two or three things you care about most deeply. The things that make you come alive, that you keep coming back to, that feel like they matter beyond just you. Now ask honestly: are these God-given desires that flow from who he made you to be — or are any of them driven by something you need to prove, achieve, or earn?
WHAT — Your Daily Life & Plans
Looking at what you actually spend your time on — your daily habits and your biggest plans — which of these genuinely feel like an expression of who God made you?
Name a few of your regular activities or major life decisions you're working toward. For each one, ask: is this flowing from a secure sense of who I am in God — an act of co-creation — or is it driven by striving, filling a gap, or performing for someone's approval? What might need to shift?
Reflection
What has been the most significant revelation of God's character you've encountered in this DTS — and what should it change?
Not the most impressive moment, but the one that actually shifted something in how you understand who God is. How should that revelation reshape your sense of who you are — your WHO? And what does it mean for the direction and decisions you're stepping into after this season?
